Large-scale digitization efforts are producing digital resources at a massive scale. Traditional manual cataloging practices can't keep up with this pace, so we have to think about how to use algorithms which can assist us to gold mine for semantics within ever-increasing corpora

Strategies for metadata creation

The LIS community has to fundamentally rethink how to create added-value metadata in contexts where it is impossible to continue with a traditional manual cataloging approach

Other communities have illustrated over the last decade how algorithms, crowdsourcing or outsourcing via micro-payments can provide alternative strategies

Companies such as Tagasauris for example are integrating machine learning for visual recognition and micro-tasks outsourced via Amazon's Mechanical Turk to provide metadata in a cheap and quick manner for the media industry.

Digital Humanities are embracing scale

DH community in particular can inspire LIS to embrace large corpora not as a problem, but as an opportunity

Methods and tools developed by the DH community to make sense out of very big volumes of non-structured text can also be re-used for automated metadata creation

Franco Moretti has been the leading thinker to elaborate the notion of distant reading. His book Maps, graphs and trees gives an excellent introduction to the topic of using statistics to analyze very large volumes of non-structured text and how the outcomes can help to answer research questions from the humanities.

Information extraction tools

Low hanging fruits from the information extraction field:

  • Named-Entity Recognition (NER)
  • Topic Modeling (TM)

Both methods can be used with a minimum of resources and technical experience. This module will give you the opportunity to experiment with both

Named-Entity Recognition (NER)

Consider the sentence :"On 25 September 2006, we visited Washington to see the White House"

  • First step => identification of entities
    • 25 September 2005
    • Washington
    • White Houwe
  • Second step => disambiguation
    • Washington: the state, the city, the jazz musician, etc ?

Disambiguate through URIs

  • Each entity is associated with a meaning :
    • http://dbpedia.org/page/White_House
    • http://dbpedia.org/page/Washington,_D.C.
  • Distinguish recognition and disambiguation :
    • Recognition is about finding the entities in a text fragment, whereas disambiguation is about mapping those entities to a universal identifier

NER services

NER services, especially the commercial services, remain very much black boxes: we throw content at them and they send back URIs with entities. From a LIS perspective, it's essential to know how to evaluate the quality of these services.
