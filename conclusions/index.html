Realize the complexity

BIBFLOW project, 2017

Be aware of people yelling Let’s get into the Linked Data cloud! These ten modules help to develop a more critical understanding of both the possibilities and the limits.

Start cleaning!

Regardless whether Linked Data will be a success or a failure, focus on what everyone should be doing: clean your metadata!

With the help of OpenRefine, you can become the deminer of your library

Linked Data is an opportunity to work on metadata quality. Based on the input of these modules you have the possibility to acquire the skills to detect issues within metadata.

However, the complexity of Linked Data should not paralyze us and be an excuse to remain at a standstill. There is a world-wide consensus about the need to fundamentally rethink library catalogs. Conceptually speaking, the RDF data model offers libraries the possibility to share their metadata and integrate them in a more intuitive manner in the Web ecology, allowing automated agents and services to re-use them. Implementing this vision on the terrain is challenging. The BIBFRAME initiative is an excellent example to illustrate the complexity.
