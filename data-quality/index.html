Putting the open-world assumption in practice implies you loose control over the quality of the data you're using. It's like publishing your birthday party as a public-event on social media. You don't know who will show up...

The Achilles Heel of Linked Data

  • As automated re-use of data rises, so does our dependency on their quality
  • Anyone interested in Linked Data should first of all come to terms with the quality of data :
    • how can you check the quality of data you want to re-use ?
    • how to audit your own data ?

A lot of books have been published on the topic, and professional and academic organisations have been launched to tackle the topic, but often badly...

Complex Topic to deal with in Practice and in Theory

  • Want to find a job? Become a data quality auditor!
  • Blooming but problematic new field
  • Examples :
    • Interpretation of climate change data : the data which indicated holes in the ozon-layer were considered at first as errouneous data
    • Information Quality Act (2000) : extremely complex to put in practice

How can we define Data Quality ?

The totality of features and characteristics
of a product, process or service that bears
on its ability to statisfy stated or implicit needs.

ISO, 2005

Commonly referred to in the literature as the "fitness for purpose" definition

A key characteristic of data quality is that we live in an empirical world, which continously changes...

Implications of living in an Empirical World

  • We can not assert a direct correspondance between the empirical, ever-changing world and the metadata and database schema representing it
  • Defining data quality in a deterministic manner (e.g. MIT Total Data Quality Program) makes no sense for empirical application domains

Difference between deterministic & empirical data

  • Deterministic data:
    • Existence of a persistent theory makes it possible to decide whether or not a value is correct
    • Example: algebra
  • Empirical data:
    • Absence of a direct frame of reference to validate data, subject to human interpretation and evolution through time
    • Example: medicine, cultural heritage sector

We can make use of Hermeneutics as a Tool to make Sense of Quality

Process of understanding moving from parts of a whole to a global understanding of the whole and back to individual parts in an iterative manner.

Klein et al, 1999

Isabelle Boydens re-used Fernand Braudel's stratified time concept as a hermeneutical approach to audit the quality of social security databases

Two key publications to understand how we can make use of hermeneutics to evaluate the quality of data.

Stratified time applied on social security databases

  • Long term
    • Evolution of policies and legislation
  • Intermediate term
    • Evolution of technologies and standards
  • Short term
    • Evolution of the objects the database documents

From Theory to Practice

  • Change is a fundamental notion to deal with...
    • Incorporate tools which help to monitor change
    • If values do not correspond to the schema, the schema itself may have to be questioned - see identification of hole in the ozone layer
    • Practice of data-profiling helps to develop data auditing skills

Getting to Grips with Data Profiling

The use of analytical techniques to discover the true structure, content and quality of a collection of data.

Olson, 2003

"There are no metadata police" Diane Hillman once famously said, but data profiling will allow you to become an investigator who can effeciently perform a data quality audit.

Recipe for any Data Quality Audit

  • We will focus on the following ingredients :
    • Flattening data
    • Column names
    • Empty columns
    • Data types
    • Lenght of entries
    • Value properties
    • Dates
    • Empty fields
    • Field overloading

Flattening data

  • Data profiling and cleaning techniques are applied outside the information system itself
  • These applications mostly ingest flat files so a conversion process might be needed

Columns as a starting point

  • Simple SQL command to create a column of a table :
    • ALTER TABLE artwork ADD title VARCHAR;
  • It is suprising how much can go wrong with
    • Name of the column
    • Data type
    • Lenght

Interpretation of Title field

  • Seems simple to interpret in the context of our art catalogue but this is not always the case
  • Download and open the Schoenberg Database of Manuscripts
    • what to expect from a field entitled Sold?
    • what is the difference between Seller and Seller2?
  • Often unused columns are re-used without renaming them

What's the Fuss with Empty Columns ?

  • Appear everywhere, often due to pre-configured settings of databases which never are modified
  • Used columns can loose their meaning at some point, but are rarely removed from the system
  • Fear of losing data results in systems with lots of unused columns

Data types

  • In our SQL example, VARCHAR was mentioned as a data type
  • Other common data types include Text, Number, Boolean or Date
  • All data types can be easily encoded with Text, makes you loose less time but things can get very messy over time

Lenght of entries

  • Seems to have losts its pertinence with decreasing storage costs
  • Text lenght of entries can be a very interesting parameter to analyse
    • If you expect the name of an individual in a field and the average text lenght is 250 characters, there might be an issue
